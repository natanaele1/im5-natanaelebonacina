How to Provide a Simplified Innovative User Experience for Gen Z Camera Users?

What is the problem?

The capability of new cameras is increasing every year, but the camera’s user interface is outdated. Camera and video camera setting menus are generally filled with hundreds of different settings and named in different ways, depending on the brand. Quickly finding a setting while you are with a client, when every second counts, can take too long. What are the UX/UI design problems and how can they be solved? The relevance of this research question is primarily social, as it addresses real-world issues faced by camera users. It also holds scientific significance in the context of human-computer interaction and user expe- rience design, contributing to the broader field of user interface design and usability studies.

Why is it important?

Efficient camera interfaces are crucial for professionals and enthusiasts who demand quick access to set- tings. Addressing these issues is pertinent to enhancing user satisfaction, especially for Generation Z. As technology evolves, the growing complexity of camera settings and menus pose challenges for users seek- ing efficient and intuitive interactions. This issue is particularly relevant for the target group, a demograph- ic known for its digital literacy and high expectations regarding user interfaces.

State of research

Current research indicates a lack of alignment between advanced camera capabilities and user needs. While some studies explore general UX/UI issues, there is a gap in brand-specific research, especially concerning Generation Z preferences. This project seeks to bridge this gap by offering tailored solutions for the specified target audience.

How do I find that out?

I will begin by researching and analyzing the Operating Systems of various cameras, to evaluate if they respect specific predetermined Design and User Interface Rules. I will generate the data for my research using a survey, asking to different users of my target audience to see what’s important.
